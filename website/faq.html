Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about DoomCode

DoomCode is a mobile app that lets you control AI coding agents (like Claude, Codex, or Gemini) from your phone. While the AI agent runs on your desktop, you can send commands, review code changes, and approve or reject modifications from anywhere using your smartphone.

DoomCode currently supports three AI coding agents:

  • Claude - Anthropic's AI assistant
  • Codex - OpenAI's coding model
  • Gemini - Google's AI model

Each agent must be installed and configured on your desktop before use with DoomCode.

Pairing is simple and secure:

  1. Run doomcode start on your desktop
  2. A QR code appears in your terminal
  3. Open the DoomCode app on your phone
  4. Scan the QR code
  5. You're connected!

The connection is established through our relay servers with end-to-end encryption, so your phone and desktop don't need to be on the same network.

Yes. All communication between your phone and desktop is end-to-end encrypted using TweetNaCl. This means:

  • Your code and commands are encrypted before leaving your device
  • Only your paired devices can decrypt the data
  • Our relay servers cannot read your messages
  • No account or login is required - your session is completely anonymous

Mobile: iOS 12.0+ and Android 8.0+

Desktop: macOS, Windows, and Linux (requires Node.js 18+)

When an AI agent proposes code changes, DoomCode shows you a diff (the differences between the old and new code). You can review exactly what the agent wants to change and either approve or reject the modification before it's applied. This gives you full control over what changes get made to your codebase.

DoomCode has built-in GitHub integration. You can securely share your GitHub token with your desktop session and create pull requests directly from the mobile app. Go to Settings in the app to configure your GitHub authentication.

DoomCode is free to download and use. You'll need your own API keys or subscriptions for the AI agents you want to control (Claude, Codex, or Gemini), as DoomCode is the interface layer, not the AI service itself.
